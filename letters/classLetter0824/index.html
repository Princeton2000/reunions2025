A Paper Letter…in the year 2024?

Sep 5, 2024 at 12:25 PM

Dear Classmate,

Yes!

Sorry, I just wanted to try that out…what a thrill! Hope you felt it too. Really, this letter is meant to catch you up on what’s going on with your Class of 2000 as we gear up for our 25th Reunion this May 22-25th. Email has been unreliable, so we’re going old school to make sure you have the most important information and can stay in touch through the year.

There are three things to remember:

  • reunions.princeton2000.org is the source of truth
  • Registration opens November 1
  • Please join our WhatsApp broadcast channel here: https://bit.ly/P2000Announcements

Reunions Site

The most important thing to remember is reunions.princeton2000.org. That will be the source of truth for all reunions information going forward. All information will be posted there first. The site is live now and has information on housing, schedule, kids activities, and frequently asked questions. Some information, like the schedule, is still developing and only directional, so check for updates throughout the year.

Registration

Our 25th Reunion will be May 22-25, 2025. That is the most important date on your calendar going forward. Just make sure you’re in Princeton those days, and everything else will pretty much work out for you. That said, the second-most important date is Nov 1, 2024, when we open registration. If you need a slightly painful point of reference, yes, that is 29 years to the day from the Early Action application deadline. Woof.

Anyway, you can go to reunions.princeton2000.org on November 1 and we will have an incredibly prominent link to registration right at the top. We’d really appreciate it if you can register early, and are offering significant discounts for those that do. The more certainty we have around attendance, the more we can commit to our entertainment, food, and beverages. If you know you’re coming, and you know you will, register early! And even if you can’t make Reunions, you can still reserve your jacket. November 1!

Communications

The University has made some unfortunate changes to Tigernet email over the last couple of years. It seems that only about 60% of you are receiving our emails, and it’s a different 60% each time. If you haven’t received our emails, I’m really sorry about that. We’re trying to address it in three ways.

First, if you set up Tigernet forwarding to your personal email years ago via @alumni.princeton.edu, that no longer works. Please go to tigernet.princeton.edu where you can set up a full-scale @alumni account, or (probably better) just update Tigernet to reflect your actual email account.

Second, while emails from Tigernet don’t seem to make it, personal emails are great, and I’m happy to send them to you on the side. Just drop me an email at jpurnell@alumni. Also, if you see an email from us that looks important, check with your friends to see if they got it. It goes a long way.

Third, we’ve set up a channel on WhatsApp that will be used for official class announcements. We all remember the great reply-all debacle of 1998, so rather than an 1,100-person group chat, it’s just us broadcasting out key information on key dates. As a side benefit, it will also give us a way to reach everyone during Reunions if we need to adjust anything on the fly. We really encourage you to join the channel via the QR code or at https://bit.ly/P2000Announcements.

You can also keep in touch through the following:

  • Facebook: https://bit.ly/P2000FacebookGroup
  • Instagram: https://bit.ly/P2000Instagram

OK. So to recap.

  • Right now: https://bit.ly/P2000Announcements.
  • November 1: Registration: reunions.princeton2000.org.
  • Anytime: reunions.princeton2000.org.
  • May 22-25: Reunions at Princeton with 2000. Org.
